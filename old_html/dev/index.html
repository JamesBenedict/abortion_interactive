A step-by-step look at abortions in Indiana

Before their 45th birthday, three in every ten women will have had an abortion. Most women seeking abortions are in their 20s and are already mothers.

While the 1992 Supreme Court case Planned Parenthood v. Casey reaffirmed a woman’s right to receive an abortion, it also gave states more rights to regulate a woman’s ability to access one.

Since the ruling went into effect, abortions in Indiana have declined by 23 percent. In 2011, 61 percent of Hoosier women lived in a county without a clinic authorized to perform abortions.

Indiana passed HR1337 in 2016, which put nine new regulations on a woman’s access to abortion. A federal judge blocked this bill a week before being enacted, but State Rep. Curt Nisly announced he will introduce bill calling for the total ban on abortion in the next legislative session.
