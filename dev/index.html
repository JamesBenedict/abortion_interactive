A step by step look at abortions in Indiana

Before their 45th birthday, three in every ten women will have had an abortion. Most women seeking abortions are in their 20s and are already mothers.

While the 1992 Supreme Court case Planned Parenthood v.Casey reaf rmed a woman’s right to receive an abortion, it also gave states more rights to regulate a woman’s ability to access one.

Since the ruling went into effect, abortions in Indiana have declined by 23 percent. In 2011, 61 percent of Hoosier women lived in a county without a clinic authorized to perform abortions.

Indiana passed HR1337 in 2016, which put nine new restrictions on a woman’s access to abortion. This interactive will introduce you to three characters stories and take you through the process of pursuing an abortion in Indiana.

Waiting period

Between your counseling appointment and your procedure, Indiana requires an 18-hour waiting period. This can be complicated by the fact that many clinics keep odd hours on only certain days of the week. To represent those 18 hours, please wait nine seconds before moving to the next step
